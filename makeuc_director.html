MakeUC Director

Background

When I started my college career at UC in fall 2020, one of the first things I did was join its chapter of IEEE, or the Institute for Electrical and Electronics Engineers. I knew of its existence from a young age thanks to my dad, who has been a professional member of the organization for as long as I can remember. Since I was accepted into the electrical engineering program, joining IEEE felt like the natural thing to do. At the end of my freshman year, I received an email asking for people to volunteer to organize MakeUC, which is the hackathon hosted by IEEE every fall. It had been held completely online the previous semester, and I had briefly attended bits and pieces of the event. It sounded like a great way to finally get more involved with the club, so I jumped at the opportunity. I was placed on the Marketing Team and spent the next several months working alongside the other organizers to plan the event. Although I did not have a leadership role, I felt like I was able to meaningfully contribute to the planning. When the opportunity came along later to be president of the club, and consequently the director of MakeUC as well, I was nervous but excited to see what I could accomplish.

The Experience

Organizing a hackathon requires more time, money, and planning than one may think. While MakeUC occurs in October every year, the organization of the event begins as early as late April or early May. Everyone who volunteers to be an organizer is assigned to one of four teams: marketing, logistics, sponsorship, or technology. Each team has its own team leader, and all of the teams are overseen by the director. My main job as director was to coordinate all of the necessary activities by organizing them into categories and disseminating them to the appropriate team(s). I was then responsible for making sure each task was assigned to team members and completed successfully. In order to fully understand everything that was going on, I opted for a hands-on approach, attending each team's meetings and doing tasks assigned to me by team leaders. Each team met every other week, and I met with the team leaders once a week.

MakeUC was also one of the most rewarding things I have ever done. One of my main goals was to make it much easier for organizers who came after us to start organizing the event each year, which I believe we accomplished by maintaining detailed meeting notes and calendars throughout the organizing process. Another thing I was happy about was the fact that I got to befriend so many of the organizers. Although we had to do a lot of the organization virtually due to people being on co-op or off-campus, I still got to know a lot of the organizers well, and I sincerely hope that they enjoyed their experience enough to consider doing it again this year. Finally, I was happy with the overall success of the event, and I am extremely excited to do it again this year (2023!). As a team, we have compiled the things that we thought went well and the things that we want to improve, and I am confident that this year's event will be even better than last year's.

As part of my experience, I wrote a short article about MakeUC for the IEEE Student Magazine, which is the student magazine published annually by IEEE at UC. Please feel free to take a look at it below to learn more about what actually happened at the event! You can also learn more about MakeUC by visiting our website, makeuc.io.
