Bearcats Academy Mentor

Background

When I was accepted into the honors program, I knew I wanted to do a self-designed experience as soon as possible. The opportunity to do so came very quickly- I heard about Bearcats Academy early on in my first semester and immediately joined. Bearcats Academy is a program that is partnered with three public high schools in Cincinnati, Ohio. The goal of the program is to pair high school students with students at the University of Cincinnati in order to form "mentor-mentee" relationships that foster learning, personal growth, and success. I am really grateful I had the opportunity to participate in the program. I learned how to be a more confident mentor and leader and how to form better relationships with others. My mentee did a fantastic job completing all of the curriculum for this year and I loved seeing her improve academically and decide to get more involved in extracurriculars. She inspired me to do the same!

The Experience

The main thing that I do as a mentor for Bearcats Academy is meet with my mentee, on average, once a week. Each session lasts an hour and covers a different topic. Each grade has a certain goal or theme to focus on for the year. Since my mentee was a freshman during the 2020-2021 school year, our focus was research. I am responsible for preparing every week by looking over the curriculums given for each session and devising a plan which goes in my mentor session log. The most recent version of that document can be found below. I chose to highlight this document because it includes all of the activities we completed and commentary on how some of the sessions went. It gives a great overview of the things we learned and accomplished this year!
