Year in Review: 2020-2021

Prompt: In what way(s) did you personally grow this year? How did you demonstrate or rebuild the resilience needed to move forward during this time? How will you utilize resources and your support network to hold yourself accountable in continuing this growth?

Unsurprisingly, my first year of college turned out to be a lot different than I imagined when I first decided I wanted to go to UC. When I realized that almost every aspect of school was going to change, I was disappointed at first, but I soon decided that I needed to make the most of it. There were a lot of new challenges, but I think that they provided me with many ways to grow as a person and as a student. Since most of my classes were online and at times asynchronous, I had to take responsibility for my learning in a new way. I had to keep track of everything I had to do and make sure I was putting in the effort to learn the material, especially for my asynchronous classes. That experience taught me that I cannot rely on others to teach me what I need to know- I need to be able to take initiative and make sure that I am understanding the content, studying, and completing any necessary work. In addition to growing as a student, I think I personally grew a lot this year. Because of the pandemic, I did not spend a lot of time with friends and family like I normally would, which taught me that I need to value the time spent with these people even more than I already do. I also found myself changing a lot socially- in past years, I was always very outgoing and wanted to talk to people whenever I could. Now, however, I find myself spending less time with fewer people and valuing my alone time more. I think that I realized the importance of maintaining the most important bonds in my life, such as with family and close friends, and not feeling the need to make as many friends or spend as much time with other people as possible. In addition to this, in past years, I found it easy to feel lonely or sad when I wasn't constantly in contact with people. Now, on the other hand, I think I have realized that even if I haven't talked to someone in a while or have been spending a lot of time by myself, the people who care about me haven't gone anywhere. Even if it does not feel like it, I'll always have people who I can talk to and who support me.

There was a lot of resilience needed to move forward throughout this year. There were periods of time in which I felt that I had no motivation to stay on top of things. There were even moments in which I felt that I was not smart enough to complete my degree. I was able to demonstrate the necessary resilience by reminding myself how lucky I am to be able to pursue a degree and receive a great education. I also reminded myself how much I desire to be an electrical engineer in the future. By keeping these sentiments in mind, I was able to keep myself motivated and consistently work hard.

In order to hold myself accountable for the continuation of the growth I experienced this year, I will utilize various resources and members of my support network. I think one of the most obvious resources is a planner- I always had my planner with me whenever I was doing work this year, and keeping track of everything in it will be something I continue to do for the foreseeable future. Another resource that I valued a lot this year was my academic advisor- she was always extremely friendly and helpful, and I know I can go to her ith any questions or concerns I may have in the future. In terms of my support network, I will be sure to continue staying in touch with my parents and my older sister while I am at school. I will also spend more time with my roommate (who also happens to be my twin sister). These things will ensure that I continue growing as a student and as a person next year and beyond.
