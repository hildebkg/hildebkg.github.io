Innovation Challenge

Background

The Innovation Challenge (IC) is a program hosted every semester (fall, spring, and summer) by CEAS Tribunal and the Center for Entrepreneurship at the University of Cincinnati. The main program, or IC 1, consists mainly of coming up with an idea for a product, making a pitch deck for the idea, creating a prototype, and presenting your pitch deck/prototype at the end of the semester to a panel of judges. This semester (fall 2023), a follow-up program called IC 2 was created. This program was meant to be a step forward for students who were already very experienced with Innovation Challenge 1 or who were really passionate about taking an idea beyond what IC 1 mainly supports. I previously participated in IC 1 in the spring with a team of fellow electrical engineering students and then did a solo project in the summer. In the spring, my team and I came up with the idea to create new-and-improved electronics kits aimed at students and hobbyists. This is the idea we carried into IC 2 this semester.

The Experience

As students and hobbyists ourselves, we had noticed that electronics kits available on the market these days were too superficial to be of use to us. There are many great kits out there that teach the basics of electronics, but because they are aimed at beginners, they do not really get into the weeds of the topics. They give a good starting point, but do not give you practical tips, tricks, and general knowledge that can be applied to a wider range of projects. When kits do cover more advanced topics, they usually assume you have the necessary prerequisite knowledge, and only briefly cover the theory and/or design criteria (if at all). It is very common to find a great kit out there, whether it be to build an AM/FM radio or build a robot car, but the common factor is that they do not explain the underlying electronics and design theory well enough. After noticing this over time, we decided we wanted to provide a solution to this problem - UpLabs! UpLabs is the name we gave to our idea for a business that creates electronics kits focused on teaching the engineering and design theory behind practical projects and that are aimed towards all audiences - complete beginners will learn everything they need to know, and those with a more experience will still benefit from the formal instruction and added theory.
IC 2 was a unique experience for which I am very grateful. The program was underway from late August until late November with meetings every Wednesday. The meeting topics varied; we would have work sessions with our teams, Q&A sessions with startup founders, team-building activities, etc. Throughout the program, we received valuable guidance from Cadence Wade, the program leader, and Dr. Jason Heikenfeld. They each gave us great advice on our idea and how to improve our understanding of the problem we were trying to solve, our business model, and our goals.
